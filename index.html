VT Camino Study Abroad

October 10 - November 18, 2016

This project was done in partnership with the Spanish department at VT. They run a 5-week trip where students walk 300km (186 miles) on a medieval pilgrimage route then spend a few more weeks living in Spain. It is a cool program and the installation has a couple elements I'm really pleased with.

Exploring Local History: Christiansburg Institute and the CI-Spy Application

August 22 - September 30, 2016

This project was a partnership with Virginia Tech's Public history department, TLOS, and the School of Visual Arts. It focused on sharing the history of a local school, called the Christiansburg Institute, founded in 1866-67 for newly emancipated African Americans.

We Thought the Future Would Be Cooler: Three Visions of 2047

April 26 - June 6, 2016

This installation was done with a graduate course in Science, Technology, and Science (STS). In the course students were challenged to envision Virginia Tech in 2047. This was in response to a new initiative by the president of Virginia Tech called Beyond Boundaries. Students challenged the notions of what the administration was putting forward and developed three small installations exploring different themes.

Innovation In & Out of the Classroom

March 25 - April 18, 2016

This exhibit featured three courses that approach innovation in different ways. These three courses also make up the core of a new innovation minor at Virginia Tech, which is part of the new Pathways for General Education.

Painting for Non-Majors

December 8 2015 - March 25 2016

Painting for Non-Majors is a course offered to all students at Virginia Tech. It is a special case because traditionally all studio art classes at Virginia Tech are restricted to art majors only. These paintings were from the first Painting for Non-Majors course.
